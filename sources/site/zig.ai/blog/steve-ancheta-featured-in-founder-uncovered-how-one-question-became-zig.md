The origin of Zig wasn't a whiteboard hypothesis. It was a phone call. Then another. Then seven.

Before building anything, Zig CEO [Steve Ancheta](https://www.linkedin.com/in/steve-ancheta-b9615541/) and his co-founders called salespeople across companies, cities, and company stages. They asked each rep to walk them through their ideal week - what they should be doing versus what they actually do. Every answer was different. Until a pattern emerged that was impossible to ignore.

Founder Uncovered sat down with Steve to trace that journey from the beginning — growing up in Boyle Heights, leaving college after one semester, spending 16 years climbing through sales roles before building the platform he wished he'd had. He talks about what it took to convince investors who didn't grow up in sales that the problem was real, why AI SDR volume without context is already a losing strategy, and the one lens he gives every early-stage founder navigating the balance between speed and direction.

_"Forward is all that matters."_
