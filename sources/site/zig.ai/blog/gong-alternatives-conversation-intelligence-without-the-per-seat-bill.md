## Chorus by ZoomInfo: Best Direct Gong Alternative for Enterprise Conversation Intelligence

Chorus offers the exact same core features as Gong. As AI call recording software at the enterprise tier, it records meetings across Zoom, Teams, and Google Meet, transcribes conversations by speaker, and tracks keywords like competitor names or pricing objections. Managers can clip call moments for coaching libraries, while the deal dashboard flags risks like skipped meetings or missing stakeholders.

#### The Main Advantage: The ZoomInfo Integration

Chorus is owned by ZoomInfo, meaning it integrates perfectly with ZoomInfo SalesOS. If your team uses ZoomInfo for prospecting, Chorus automatically pulls in buyer titles, company intent signals, and past outreach history right into the call view.

For teams reliant on ZoomInfo data, this native connection is the main reason to choose Chorus over Gong.

#### The Disadvantages

- **The Bundled Pricing:** Chorus is rarely sold as a standalone tool anymore. It is usually bundled into the broader ZoomInfo platform, which means you may have to pay for extra software features you do not want or need.
- **Analysis, Not Action:** Just like Gong, Chorus only analyzes your calls; it does not automate the work that follows. Your sales reps still have to update the CRM manually, write their own follow-up emails, and log their own next-step tasks. Chorus tells management what happened, but it does not save the rep any post-call admin time. As conversation intelligence software, it stops at analysis.

#### Best For

- Mid-market to enterprise teams (roughly 75+ reps) where ZoomInfo SalesOS is already the prospecting backbone.
- Sales orgs running structured coaching programs with dedicated enablement headcount.
- Teams that need consistent methodology adherence tracking (MEDDIC, BANT, Command of the Message).
- RevOps functions that want one vendor across prospecting data and conversation intelligence.
 
 ### Clari Copilot: Best Gong Alternative for Forecasting-Connected Conversation Intelligence
 

Clari Copilot provides standard conversation intelligence features like call recording and transcription, but it stands out by focusing on real-time guidance and forecasting data. Among revenue intelligence tools, it's the one with the tightest forecast integration.

#### The Main Advantage: Real-Time Coaching and Revenue Forecasting

Unlike AI sales coaching tools that only analyze calls after they happen, Copilot coaches reps during live meetings. As a call unfolds, it automatically surfaces battlecards when competitors are mentioned, prompts reps with scripts to handle pricing objections, and reminds them to ask skipped qualification questions.

Additionally, these call signals feed directly into Clari's forecasting engine, meaning an AE's discovery call on Tuesday instantly updates the revenue numbers leadership reviews on Friday.

#### The Disadvantages

- **Dormant Features Without Clari Forecasting:** Copilot's best feature is its native connection to Clari's forecasting platform. If your team does not run structured forecast calls through Clari, you are paying a premium for a forecasting integration you cannot use.
- **Complex Enterprise Pricing:** While Copilot can be purchased as a reasonably priced standalone tool, bundling it into the full Clari platform pushes it into expensive enterprise pricing territory.

#### Best For

- Mid-market and enterprise sales orgs (50+ reps) where the weekly forecast call is a structured ritual.
- Teams running MEDDPICC, MEDDIC, or Command of the Sale who want methodology adherence enforced in real time.
- Orgs with a high SDR-to-AE ramp volume—the live coaching shortens time-to-productivity measurably.
- RevOps functions that already own Clari and want CI from the same vendor.
- Sales leaders who care more about forecast accuracy than coaching library depth.

### Summaries Aren't the Endpoint Anymore

The 2024-era pitch was "AI summarizes your calls." That problem is solved. Every tool on this list does it. The current bottleneck is what happens after the summary lands.

Stage doesn't update. MEDDIC fields stay empty. The follow-up email gets pushed to tomorrow. Pipeline data degrades over a quarter.

Teams shopping for Gong alternatives in 2026 are usually past the "we need transcripts" conversation. They're asking which tool closes the loop between the call ending and the CRM record being current.

### The CRM Update Problem Isn't One Problem

It's three, and most CRM update automation tools solve only one:

Knowing which of the three is the actual pain narrows the shortlist faster than any feature comparison.

Most tools on this list assume the rep is at their laptop. Zig accepts commands from mobile, voice, Slack, email, and phone, which is the differentiation if the above describes your motion. As a mobile-first sales tool, it's currently the only one of the ten that's built for this workflow rather than retrofitted to it. A tool that lives in the manager's dashboard generates insight. A tool that lives in the rep's Slack and CRM generates leverage. The best Slack sales assistant is the one reps already have open, not the one they have to remember to log into.

## Conversation Intelligence vs Sales Execution

The market for conversation intelligence tools is undergoing a massive shift. It is no longer just about recording meetings; it is about deciding whether you need a tool that simply observes your deals or one that actively pushes them forward.

To choose the right software, you need to understand how the category has split into two distinct layers:

### Conversation Intelligence: Checking What Already Happened

Traditional Conversation Intelligence (CI) is focused entirely on observation. It takes a recorded meeting and extracts structured data from it. This includes:

- Word-for-word transcripts and basic bullet-point summaries.
- Trackers for competitor mentions, pricing objections, and missed qualification questions.
- Dashboard metrics like talk-to-listen ratios and buyer sentiment scores.

This is the playground of platforms like Gong, Chorus, and Clari Copilot. They are excellent at giving sales managers high-level visibility and coaching data. The catch is that their output is purely descriptive. Once the call ends, the software stops working, and the actual sales rep still has to do all the manual data entry by hand.

### Sales Execution: AI That Handles the Grunt Work

The newer Sales Execution layer is built around a completely different question:

What chores can we automate the second a call finishes?

Instead of just providing a summary for a manager to read, an execution tool carries out the administrative tasks that usually eat up a rep's afternoon. These tools can:

- **Autofill CRM Data:** Automatically update deal stages, log next-step tasks, and fill out complex tracking frameworks (like MEDDPICC) using direct quotes and citations from the call.
- **Draft Unique Follow-Ups:** Instantly generate follow-up emails written in the rep's specific professional voice, trained on their past sent history.
- **Trigger Revenue Workflows:** Instantly route a competitive alert to product marketing or flag a deal as a churn risk in Slack the moment a customer voices a compliance concern.

Platforms like Zig and Sybill live in this category. They shift the software's job from a read-only dashboard to a read-and-write assistant.

### The Modern Playbook: Consolidate or Pair

For large sales organizations, this isn't an either/or choice. Enterprise teams frequently build a dual stack: they keep a traditional CI platform like Gong for high-level management tracking, and layer an execution tool like Zig on top to save their reps from data-entry burnout.

However, if you run a growing team of under 30 reps, buying both is usually a waste of budget. At that scale, heavy management dashboards aren't the bottleneck, execution capacity is. Smaller teams are increasingly skipping traditional conversation intelligence entirely, opting for a single execution-first tool to handle both the call capture and the post-call admin in one go.
