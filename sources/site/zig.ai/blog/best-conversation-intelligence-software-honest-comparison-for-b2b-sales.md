## What Is Conversation Intelligence Software?

**Conversation intelligence software** records, transcribes, and analyzes sales conversations so teams can understand buyer intent, objections, risks, next steps, and coaching opportunities. The best platforms also connect those insights to CRM updates, follow-up workflows, deal reviews, and sales execution.

The category has matured well past basic call recording. What separates strong conversation intelligence software from a generic meeting recorder is the sales-specific layer on top of the transcript: objection detection, deal risk identification, competitive mentions, next-step suggestions, and CRM data updates.

### **Conversation intelligence vs. AI meeting notes**

AI meeting notetakers summarize what happened. Conversation intelligence identifies sales-specific signals within the conversation. The outputs are different: notetakers produce summaries; conversation intelligence platforms produce objections, competitor mentions, buying committee details, pricing concerns, next steps, call scores, and deal risk assessments. For B2B sales teams, that difference matters.

### **Conversation intelligence vs. revenue intelligence**

Conversation intelligence analyzes sales conversations. Revenue intelligence connects conversation data to pipeline health, forecasting, deal management, and revenue decisions. The distinction is covered in detail in the [revenue intelligence explainer](https://zig.ai/blog/revenue-intelligence-what-it-is-why-it-matters-and-how-its-different-from-crm), useful reading for teams deciding which layer they actually need.

## How to Choose the Right Conversation Intelligence Software

Choosing conversation intelligence software comes down to understanding what the team actually needs after the call ends, not just what the tool promises during the demo.

Here's a practical buying framework: Audit where reps lose time after calls: CRM entry, follow-up drafting, next-step creation Check CRM update requirements: Does the platform push notes or update structured fields? Map the sales motion: outbound, inbound, SaaS demos, enterprise AE, expansion, or renewals Compare AI features beyond summaries Evaluate mobile requirements. Calculate total cost, including seats, implementation, unused licenses, and tools replaced. Test rep adoption before committing to a full rollout. Review data security, permissions, and recording consent requirements. Choose the tool that removes the most real work

### **Questions to ask vendors**

- Can the software update CRM fields, or does it only push notes?
- Which CRMs are fully supported?
- Does it draft follow-up emails?
- Can reps approve or reject AI-generated actions?
- Does it work on mobile beyond playback?
- How does pricing scale as the team grows?
- What tools can it realistically replace?
- How long does implementation take?
- What happens to data if reps don't log in consistently?

Recording consent and data governance also deserve attention. Requirements vary by jurisdiction, and responsible AI governance frameworks like the [NIST AI Risk Management Framework](https://www.nist.gov/itl/ai-risk-management-framework) provide a useful baseline for thinking about approval controls, audit logs, and permissions. For legal guidance on recording consent specifically, the [DMLP legal guide on recording phone calls and conversations](https://www.dmlp.org/legal-guide/recording-phone-calls-and-conversations) is a practical reference.

_Verify jurisdictional requirements with legal counsel before deploying any recording tool._
