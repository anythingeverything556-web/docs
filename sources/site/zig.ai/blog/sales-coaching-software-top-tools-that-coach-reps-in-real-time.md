## What Is Sales Coaching Software?

Sales coaching software helps sales managers review rep performance, analyze calls, surface coaching moments, track skill development, and standardize follow-up. Modern platforms often include conversation intelligence, AI call summaries, scorecards, real-time prompts, CRM sync, and coaching workflows.

The category spans a wide range, from lightweight meeting recorders to full revenue intelligence platforms. What separates useful sales coaching software from a recording tool is the layer above the transcript: objection detection, talk-ratio analysis, deal risk flags, coaching scorecards, and structured workflows that connect coaching moments to rep behavior.

| Feature | What It Does | Why It Matters |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Call recording | Captures sales conversations | Managers can review real examples instead of relying on rep memory |
| Transcription | Turns calls into searchable text | Coaching moments become easier to find |
| AI call summaries | Summarizes what happened on the call | Reps and managers save review time |
| Scorecards | Grades calls against defined criteria | Coaching becomes more consistent |
| Talk-time analysis | Shows rep vs buyer balance | Helps managers spot conversation patterns |
| Objection tracking | Identifies recurring objections | Managers can coach messaging and positioning |
| Keyword detection | Flags competitor, pricing, budget, and timeline mentions | Important moments are easier to inspect |
| Real-time prompts | Guides reps during live calls | Reps can adjust while the conversation is still happening |
| CRM sync | Logs summaries, notes, and activities | Coaching connects to pipeline context |
| Follow-up support | Drafts or tracks next steps | Coaching turns into action |

## Which Sales Coaching Software Gives Real-Time Call Feedback?

The sales coaching tools most associated with real-time call feedback are platforms that listen to live calls and surface prompts, battlecards, objection guidance, or knowledge snippets during the conversation. Teams should separate true live coaching from post-call AI coaching and call-summary automation.

Research into [real-time AI sales support](https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.21416) shows that live question detection and answer retrieval during calls can improve rep confidence and reduce reliance on memory during complex technical conversations. That research also notes that the value depends heavily on the quality of the underlying knowledge base and how prompts are surfaced without disrupting the call.

| Coaching Type | What It Does | Best Fit | Limitation |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Live call prompts | Shows guidance while the rep is still on the call | Teams that want in-call support | Can distract reps if too noisy |
| Real-time answer retrieval | Finds approved answers to buyer questions during calls | Technical or product-heavy sales teams | Requires strong internal knowledge base |
| Manager live alerts | Flags calls that may need manager attention | SDR teams and call centers | Works best in high-volume environments |
| Post-call coaching | Scores calls and surfaces coaching moments after the call | Most B2B sales teams | Feedback may arrive too late for that call |
| AI call summaries | Summarizes calls and action items | Reps and managers who need faster review | Summary quality depends on transcript accuracy |
| Coaching plus execution | Turns coaching signals into CRM updates, follow-ups, and next steps | Teams that want coaching to change pipeline behavior | Needs CRM integration and workflow design |
