## FAQ

If you have additional questions, please don't hesitate  to contact our team.

How does Zig's pricing work?

Zig pricing has two components that customers typically experience as one predictable monthly bill: a flat Platform Fee (covering integrations, orchestration, monitoring, and ongoing operational responsibility) and an Execution Charge (sized to the actual workload your team generates). You're not paying per seat, per action, or per token. You're paying for Zig to take on real execution responsibility.

What plans are available?

Zig offers three plans designed around different team sizes and execution needs. Pay-As-You-Go is the baseline: best for individuals or very small teams of 1–2 reps who want to get started quickly. Growth is the recommended plan for most sales teams: designed for teams of 3 or more, with significantly discounted per-action pricing, unlimited users, and unlimited features. Enterprise is built for larger organizations with 30+ reps, where Zig becomes the execution layer across teams or departments, with custom pricing, dedicated support, and infrastructure-grade reliability.

What's included in every plan?

Every plan includes full platform access with all Zig capabilities. There are no feature gates, no tiered packages, and no usage caps on features. The plans differ primarily by per-action pricing and the level of onboarding support. As we ship new features, they're available to everyone automatically.

Why isn't Zig priced per seat?

Because seats don't reflect the work Zig does. A team of 10 reps with heavy pipeline activity generates more execution work than a team of 30 who use it lightly. Zig is sized to the workload it absorbs — not the number of logins. This also means customers aren't penalized for adoption.

What happens as my team or usage grows?

Temporary spikes are absorbed without any changes. If your team's workload sustainably increases, it becomes a conversation; not a surprise bill. Overages are a safety valve for real growth, not a mechanism to meter adoption.

How does Zig compare to the cost of ops headcount?

Most sales reps spend 25–40% of their time on busywork across multiple tools and software. Zig absorbs the majority of that. Compared to hiring even one RevOps or Sales Ops hire, Zig is typically a fraction of the cost — and runs 24/7. The ROI conversation usually anchors on time and labor, not projected revenue lift.

Is there a trial or pilot option?

We offer a Proof of Value engagement for our customers, where we work with your team to demonstrate measurable impact before you commit long-term.
