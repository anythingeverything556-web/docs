Call your dedicated Zig number, say what needs to happen, and your AI agent takes care of it. No laptop, no app, no pulling over to type a note. Voice commands let you keep your CRM current, trigger follow-ups, and query your pipeline from anywhere — driving between meetings, walking out of a building, or sitting in an airport gate.

## How Voice Commands Work

Every Zig workspace comes with a dedicated phone number tied to your AI agent. Dialing that number connects you directly to your agent, which listens, understands context, and acts — or surfaces an approval card if the action needs your sign-off before it goes out.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Dial your dedicated Zig number">
    Call the Zig number assigned to your workspace. You'll connect to your personal AI agent immediately — no menus, no hold music.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Speak your command naturally">
    Say what you want done in plain language. Your agent understands deal names, contact names, CRM stages, and natural phrasing — you don't need to follow a script.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Zig executes or sends an approval card">
    Simple internal updates happen automatically. Actions that involve sending something externally — an email, a calendar invite, a LinkedIn message — surface as approval cards in your app, email, or Slack for a quick review before they go out.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## What You Can Do by Voice

| What you say | What happens |
|---|---|
| "Move Acme to Proposal Sent." | CRM deal stage updates immediately. |
| "Send the follow-up to Marcus about pricing on Friday." | Zig drafts the message and sends an approval card for your review. |
| "Call Emily and offer her times from my calendar." | Zig checks your availability and queues a scheduling action for approval. |
| "What's the status of the Globex deal?" | Zig reads back the current stage, last activity, and next step. |
| "Log my meeting with Sarah at TechCorp." | Zig logs the activity to the CRM record immediately. |

## Automatic vs. Approval Actions

<Tabs>
  <Tab title="Executed Automatically">
    These actions are low-risk and internal — your agent handles them instantly without requiring a review.

    - **CRM field updates** — stage changes, owner reassignments, custom field edits
    - **Activity logging** — calls, meetings, and notes written to the CRM record
    - **Pipeline queries** — reading back deal status, next steps, or contact details
    - **Internal task creation** — adding reminders or to-dos tied to a deal or contact

    You can review any automatic action in the activity feed after it runs.
  </Tab>
  <Tab title="Requires Your Approval">
    These actions reach outside your organization or commit something on your behalf — so Zig always surfaces them for review first.

    - **Outbound emails** — any message sent to a prospect or customer
    - **LinkedIn messages** — outreach or follow-ups sent via LinkedIn
    - **Meeting invitations** — calendar invites sent to external contacts
    - **Scheduled calls** — any action that books time with a contact
    - **Sequences and campaigns** — enrolling a contact in a multi-step outreach flow

    Approval cards appear in your Zig app, inbox, or Slack — wherever you're most likely to see them. One tap to approve, edit, or dismiss.
  </Tab>
</Tabs>

<Note>
  Voice commands are processed by your personal AI agent and respect the same approval rules as actions triggered from the app or Slack. Changing your approval settings in **Settings → Approvals** applies to voice, app, and Slack actions equally.
</Note>

<Tip>
  Use voice commands in the two minutes between meetings to keep your CRM current. By the time you're back at your desk, every stage update, call log, and queued follow-up is already done — no catch-up required at end of day.
</Tip>
