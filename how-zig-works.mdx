Zig runs on a multi-agent architecture where each AI agent is purpose-built for a specific part of the sales workflow. Rather than a single general-purpose assistant, Zig deploys six specialized agents — each expert in its domain — that coordinate automatically so every action flows into the next without you stitching anything together.

## Multi-Agent Orchestration

Each stage of your sales motion has a dedicated AI agent. When you come out of a discovery call, your Meetings agent hands context to your Follow-Up agent, which drafts the email, while your CRM Sync agent logs the notes — all in parallel, without waiting on you.

Here's what each agent owns:

- **Research Agent** — Account and contact intelligence. Surfaces firmographic data, recent news, CRM history, and buying signals before every touch.
- **Outreach Agent** — Personalized sequencing. Drafts multi-step outreach in your voice, calibrated to each prospect's context and stage.
- **Meetings Agent** — Pre-meeting preparation. Generates briefs from CRM data, past interactions, open deal status, and suggested talk tracks.
- **Follow-Up Agent** — Post-meeting execution. ZigScribe listens to your calls live and auto-drafts follow-up emails and action items the moment the call ends.
- **CRM Sync Agent** — Automated logging. Every note, status change, and next step is written back to your CRM with 95% accuracy, hands-free.
- **Pipeline Agent** — Deal health monitoring. Tracks every open opportunity, flags stale deals, and surfaces risk before it affects your forecast.

All six agents share a common intelligence layer, so what one agent learns — a stakeholder's preference, a deal's risk signal, a rep's winning sequence — informs every other agent's actions going forward.

## The Intelligence Layer

Zig doesn't just execute tasks — it builds organizational memory from every interaction across your sales org. Every call ZigScribe transcribes, every email Zig drafts, and every deal outcome it observes feeds a continuously improving model of how your team wins.

Over time, this intelligence layer enables Zig to move beyond execution and into judgment:

- It learns which accounts match your best closed-won customers and surfaces lookalikes after a new win.
- It identifies which rep behaviors, talk tracks, and follow-up timing patterns correlate with faster closes.
- It detects when a deal is going quiet — based on engagement signals — before you notice the deal slipping in your pipeline.
- It calibrates every agent's output to your specific business: your ICPs, your product, your language, your motion.

This is what makes Zig a compounding asset rather than a one-time productivity tool — the longer it runs, the sharper its outputs become.

## How the Rep Stays in Control

Zig is built on the principle that automation should accelerate your judgment, not replace it. Every action Zig takes falls into one of two modes:

<Tabs>
  <Tab title="Automatic Actions">
    Simple, routine, and low-risk actions execute automatically — no approval needed. Zig handles these in the background so you never have to think about them.

    **Examples of automatic actions:**
    - Logging a call summary to your CRM after a meeting ends
    - Updating deal stage based on activity signals
    - Generating a pre-meeting brief and delivering it to your inbox before a scheduled call
    - Flagging a stale deal in your pipeline digest
    - Enriching a new contact record with firmographic data
  </Tab>
  <Tab title="Approval Required">
    Complex, outbound, or high-stakes actions surface as approval cards — one tap to approve, or a quick edit before they go out. You stay in full control of anything that goes to a prospect or changes a deal's trajectory.

    **Examples of actions that require approval:**
    - Sending a follow-up email to a prospect after a call
    - Launching a new outreach sequence to a target account
    - Updating a deal's close date or forecast category
    - Sending a meeting recap to multiple stakeholders
    - Triggering a re-engagement sequence for a stalled deal

    Approval cards appear wherever you work — in Slack, email, ZigMobile, or the Zig desktop app. Review, edit if needed, and tap once to execute.
  </Tab>
</Tabs>

## Frequently Asked Questions

<Accordion title="Does Zig replace my CRM?">
  No. Zig works on top of your existing CRM — it doesn't replace it. Zig connects to Salesforce and other supported platforms and writes data back automatically. Your CRM stays the system of record; Zig is the execution layer that keeps it accurate and up to date without any manual effort from your reps.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Do my reps have to learn a new tool?">
  No. Zig is designed to work through the tools your reps already use every day — email, Slack, and phone. Reps receive AI-drafted outputs and approval cards in their existing workflows and never need to open a new tab or learn a new interface. The ZigMobile app and desktop workspace are available for reps who want deeper visibility, but neither is required to get full value from Zig.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="How quickly does Zig start doing useful work?">
  Most reps see their first Zig output within 24 hours of connecting their CRM. On day one, Zig can already generate pre-meeting briefs, surface account research, and begin drafting follow-ups. The quality of outputs improves continuously as Zig learns more about your deals, your language, and your sales motion.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="What if I want to review an action before it goes out?">
  All outbound and high-stakes actions require one-tap approval before they execute. Zig surfaces these as approval cards in Slack, email, ZigMobile, or the desktop app — wherever you're working at the time. You can review the draft, make edits, and approve with a single tap. Nothing goes to a prospect without your sign-off.
</Accordion>
