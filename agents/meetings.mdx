ZigScribe is Zig's AI meeting intelligence layer — your preparation partner, your in-call listener, and your post-call summarizer, all in one. It works quietly in the background so you can focus on the conversation instead of note-taking, and it makes sure nothing important slips through the cracks between "let's talk" and "send me the contract."

## What ZigScribe Does

ZigScribe begins working before the call starts. The night before a scheduled meeting, it assembles a meeting-ready brief: buyer context pulled from your CRM and recent research, open questions from prior conversations, and key talking points tailored to the account. You walk in knowing what matters to this buyer, what's been discussed before, and where this deal stands.

During the call, ZigScribe listens quietly and monitors for signals that change what you should do next — objections that need addressing, buying signals worth following up on, and competitor mentions that require a response. You get real-time context without having to track it yourself.

When the call ends, your summary and next steps are ready. ZigScribe produces a structured post-call output that feeds directly into the Follow-Up agent and the CRM Sync agent, so nothing needs to be re-entered or manually written up.

## How to Use ZigScribe

<Steps>
  <Step title="Connect your calendar">
    Go to **Settings → Integrations** and connect your Google Calendar or Outlook Calendar. ZigScribe automatically detects upcoming meetings with external contacts and begins prep.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Review your meeting brief">
    The night before each meeting, open the ZigScribe brief from your Zig dashboard or the mobile app. Read through the buyer context, open questions, and talking points. Add your own notes if needed.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Start the meeting">
    Join your call as normal. ZigScribe joins automatically (or you can admit it from the waiting room if required). No special setup needed during the call — it works in the background.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Acknowledge live signals">
    If ZigScribe surfaces a buying signal or objection during the call, a subtle in-app indicator appears. Tap to expand context or dismiss — it won't interrupt your conversation.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Review your post-call summary">
    When the call ends, your summary, action items, and next steps are ready in your Zig dashboard. Approve the suggested CRM updates and hand off to the Follow-Up agent with one tap.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Example

**Scenario:** You have a discovery call tomorrow with a VP of Finance at a Series C fintech company. The night before, ZigScribe surfaces a brief: the company recently announced a headcount freeze, the contact previously engaged with your pricing page, and two open questions remain from a prior email exchange. You review it in five minutes before bed. On the call, you open strong by addressing the budget constraint directly — and ZigScribe flags a buying signal when the contact asks about contract flexibility. Post-call, your summary and action items are ready in three minutes.

## Tips

<Tip>
  Review your meeting brief on mobile the morning of the call, not just the night before. A quick refresh while commuting helps the key points stick and lets you spot anything that changed overnight (like a news alert on the prospect's company).
</Tip>

<Tip>
  Share the post-call summary with your prospect as a follow-up — "here's what we covered and what we agreed to do next." It builds trust, confirms alignment, and creates a paper trail that accelerates the deal.
</Tip>
