Most deals don't announce when they're dying — they just go quiet. Zig's Pipeline agent watches every active deal across your CRM and outreach activity, surfaces risk signals before a deal slips off your radar, and hands you a drafted re-engagement message before you've even noticed the gap. For leaders, it provides a live read on what's moving and what needs attention — without chasing reps for updates.

## What the Pipeline Agent Does

The Pipeline agent monitors deal activity across your entire book of business in real time. It tracks engagement recency, stage progression, and communication patterns, then flags deals that are going quiet, stuck, or showing signs of churn risk. You get an early warning — not a post-mortem.

When the agent identifies a stalled deal, it doesn't just alert you. It finds the right next move and drafts it: a re-engagement email, a check-in message, a new value-add to share. You approve and send, or edit and send. The draft means you spend ten seconds on a problem that would otherwise sit in the back of your mind for days.

For revenue leaders and sales managers, the Pipeline agent provides deal-level visibility without requiring updates from reps. You see which deals are advancing, which are at risk, and which need a coaching conversation — all from a single view that reflects real activity, not self-reported status.

## How to Use the Pipeline Agent

<Steps>
  <Step title="Connect your CRM">
    Make sure your CRM is connected under **Settings → Integrations**. The Pipeline agent pulls deal data directly from Salesforce or your connected CRM to monitor stage, activity, and engagement.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Set your risk thresholds">
    Go to **Settings → Agents → Pipeline** and configure when a deal should be flagged as at-risk — for example, no activity for 7 days in Proposal stage, or no reply for 5 days after a demo. You can set thresholds by stage or apply defaults.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Review your daily deal digest">
    Each morning, open the Pipeline dashboard or check your Zig daily digest. Flagged deals appear at the top with a risk reason (e.g., "No contact in 11 days — last touch was pricing email") and a drafted re-engagement message.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Approve or customize re-engagement drafts">
    For each flagged deal, review the drafted outreach. Edit for tone or specifics if needed, then approve. Zig schedules and sends through your connected channel.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Use leader view for pipeline reviews">
    In the **Team Pipeline** tab, managers see all active deals by rep — with risk indicators, last activity dates, and engagement scores. Use this view in weekly pipeline reviews instead of asking reps to update their forecasts before the meeting.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Example

<Note>
  **From a Zig user:** "Zig flagged a deal I hadn't touched in 11 days before I even noticed. Draft re-engagement was already waiting."
</Note>

**Scenario:** You're carrying 22 active opportunities across three stages. On a Tuesday morning, the Pipeline agent surfaces three alerts: one deal has had no contact in 11 days, one has been in Proposal stage for three weeks without a stage change, and one contact opened your pricing deck five times yesterday — a buying signal worth acting on. For each, Zig has a drafted next step. You review all three in under five minutes, approve two messages and edit one, and get back to your morning. Without the agent, the stalled deals might have sat untouched for another week.

## Tips

<Tip>
  Tune your risk thresholds during your first week of use. If you get too many alerts, you'll ignore them. Start conservative (longer inactivity windows) and tighten as you learn which signals actually predict deal risk in your sales cycle.
</Tip>

<Tip>
  For sales leaders: share the Team Pipeline view with your reps before your weekly pipeline review. When everyone can see the same deal-health data ahead of the meeting, the conversation shifts from "what's the status?" to "what's the plan?" — which is where the real coaching happens.
</Tip>
