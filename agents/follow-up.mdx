The 48 hours after a meeting are when most pipeline value is won or lost. Buyers are engaged, the conversation is fresh, and the next step is still clear — but only if you move fast. Zig's Follow-Up agent closes that window automatically, drafting recaps and action items from the actual conversation before you've had time to open a new tab.

## What the Follow-Up Agent Does

The Follow-Up agent picks up immediately where ZigScribe leaves off. The moment a call ends, it uses the meeting transcript and summary to draft a post-meeting recap email — including a summary of what was discussed, the action items each party owns, and a clear next step. You review, approve, and send. What used to take 45 minutes happens in three.

Beyond post-call follow-up, the agent monitors your active threads for silence. When a buyer goes quiet — no reply after an email, no callback after a voicemail, no acknowledgment after a proposal — the agent detects the gap and drafts a re-engagement message tailored to the context. It catches no-shows, voicemails, and dropped threads before they become lost deals.

The Follow-Up agent is designed around the principle that most deals don't die from a hard "no" — they die from drift. Consistent, well-timed follow-up keeps your deal in the buyer's field of attention and signals that you're the kind of partner who follows through.

## How to Use the Follow-Up Agent

<Steps>
  <Step title="Enable post-call automation">
    In **Settings → Agents → Follow-Up**, toggle on automatic post-call drafting. Choose whether you want drafts sent automatically after approval or queued for manual review.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Review the post-meeting draft">
    Within minutes of your call ending, open the Follow-Up agent in your dashboard. Review the drafted recap, edit any action items or tone, and tap **Send** to deliver it to your contact.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Set your silence thresholds">
    Configure how long Zig should wait before flagging an unresponsive thread — for example, 48 hours after a proposal, 72 hours after a demo. You can set defaults or customize per deal stage.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Approve re-engagement drafts">
    When the agent detects silence, it surfaces a re-engagement draft in your queue. Review the message (Zig uses prior conversation context to keep it relevant), approve, and send.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Manage no-shows">
    If a scheduled meeting is missed, Zig automatically queues a no-show follow-up draft within the hour. You can customize the tone (light touch vs. direct rescheduling ask) in your agent settings.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Tips

<Tip>
  Customize your silence thresholds by deal stage. A prospect in early discovery can wait 72 hours before re-engagement; a prospect who's had the proposal for 48 hours without a word deserves a nudge sooner.
</Tip>

<Tip>
  Always include a clear next step in every follow-up draft before approving. Zig drafts one by default, but verify it matches what was actually agreed on the call — specificity ("Thursday at 2 PM" vs. "sometime this week") significantly improves reply rates.
</Tip>
