Zig's Outreach agent replaces the grind of manually drafting, scheduling, and sending messages across channels with a single command in plain English. Tell Zig who to contact, what the message is about, and when to send it — and an approval card is waiting for you before you've moved on to your next task. You stay in control of every send while the agent handles the work.

## What the Outreach Agent Does

The Outreach agent operates across email, LinkedIn, and SMS from a single interface. Instead of switching between tools and copy-pasting context, you describe what you want to send and Zig drafts a personalized message using everything it knows about the account — past interactions, company signals, and your playbook. You review, approve with one tap, and move on.

Sequences are built around account context and conversation history, so each touchpoint feels relevant rather than generic. The agent handles timing, spacing between steps, and channel selection based on what's most likely to get a response. You can also build and save multi-step sequences for common scenarios like product demos, pricing follow-ups, or re-engagement campaigns.

On Growth Team and above plans, Zig includes AI-automated LinkedIn outreach, extending your reach on the channel where many buyers spend their day — without requiring you to manually log in and compose each message.

## How to Use the Outreach Agent

<Steps>
  <Step title="Connect your sending channels">
    Go to **Settings → Integrations** and connect your Gmail or Outlook account. Optionally connect LinkedIn for automated outreach (available on Growth Team and above) and SMS if enabled on your workspace.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Give Zig a command">
    Open the Zig command bar and type what you want to send. Be as natural as you like: "Send Priya at CloudNova a cold intro email this Tuesday morning" or "Follow up with the Acme team about their security questions."
  </Step>
  <Step title="Review the approval card">
    Zig surfaces a draft approval card with the message, channel, recipient, and scheduled send time. Read it over, edit inline if needed, and tap **Approve** to confirm.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Monitor sequence performance">
    Track open rates, reply rates, and next steps in your outreach dashboard. Zig flags contacts who haven't responded so you can adjust timing or channel without hunting through your inbox.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Build reusable sequences">
    Save high-performing outreach flows as named sequences (e.g., "Demo Follow-Up — Enterprise"). Apply them to new contacts from the Research agent with one click.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Example

<Note>
  **From a Zig user:** "I said 'send Marcus a follow-up about pricing on Friday.' The approval card was waiting for me. One tap."
</Note>

**Scenario:** You finish a discovery call with a VP of Operations at a mid-market logistics company. While closing your notes, you tell Zig: "Send David a follow-up with our ROI one-pager and ask if Thursday works for a pricing call." Zig drafts an email using the call context, attaches the right asset, and presents the approval card. You approve in ten seconds. Two days later, Zig flags that David hasn't opened the email and suggests a LinkedIn touchpoint — one more tap, one more approval, done.

## Tips

<Tip>
  Be specific in your commands. Mentioning the contact's name, the subject matter, and a send date helps Zig personalize the draft and schedule accurately without needing follow-up clarification.
</Tip>

<Tip>
  Use the approval flow as a quality checkpoint, not a chore. A quick read before sending catches tone mismatches and lets you add a personal line that makes the message feel handcrafted — even when Zig did the heavy lifting.
</Tip>
